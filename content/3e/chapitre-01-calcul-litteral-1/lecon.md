<h2 class="subsection-title">1.1 Distributivité simple</h2>

<h3 class="subsubsection-title">1.1.1 Développer une expression littérale</h3>

<div class="environment definition">
<div class="environment-title">Définition :</div>
<div class="environment-content">
Développer une expression, c'est transformer un produit en somme.
</div>
</div>

<div class="environment exemple">
<div class="environment-title">Exemple</div>
<div class="environment-content">
Cela peut aider pour calculer astucieusement : <br>
${\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{24}} \times 101 = {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{24}} \times (100 + 1) = {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{24}} \times 100 + {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{24}} \times 1 = 2400 + 24 = 2424$
</div>
</div>

<div class="environment propriete">
<div class="environment-title">Propriété - Distributivité simple</div>
<div class="environment-content">
On considère $a, b, k$ trois nombres.

<ul style="column-count:2; column-gap:2rem;">
<li>$ k \times (a + b) = k \times a + k \times b $</li>
<li>$ k \times (a - b) = k \times a - k \times b $</li>
</ul>

</div>
</div>

<div class="environment exemple">
<div class="environment-title">Exemples</div>
<div class="environment-content">

<div class="math-display" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:top; width:calc(49% - 10px); margin:0 5px 5px 0;">
$$
\begin{align*}
\quad {\textcolor{#EB7F73}{\bullet}} \quad {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{2}}(x + 3) &= {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{2}} \times x + {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{2}} \times 3 &\\
&= 2x + 6 &\end{align*}
$$
</div><div class="math-display" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:top; width:calc(49% - 10px); margin:0 5px 5px 0;">
$$
\begin{align*}
\quad {\textcolor{#EB7F73}{\bullet}} \quad {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{8y}}(2y - 3) &= {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{8y}} \times 2y - {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{8y}} \times 3& \\
&= 16y^2 - 24& \end{align*}
$$
</div>

</div>
</div>

<h3 class="subsubsection-title">1.1.2 Factoriser une expression</h3>

<div class="environment definition">
<div class="environment-title">Définition :</div>
<div class="environment-content">
Factoriser une expression, c'est transformer une somme en un produit.
</div>
</div>

<div class="environment propriete">
<div class="environment-title">Propriété - Factorisation</div>
<div class="environment-content">
On considère $a, b, k$ trois nombres.

<ul style="column-count:2; column-gap:2rem;">
<li>$ k \times a + k \times b = k \times (a + b) $</li>
<li>$ k \times a - k \times b = k \times (a - b) $</li>
</ul>

</div>
</div>

<div class="environment exemple">
<div class="environment-title">Exemples</div>
<div class="environment-content">

<div class="math-display" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:top; width:calc(49% - 10px); margin:0 5px 5px 0;">
$$
\begin{align*}
\quad {\textcolor{#EB7F73}{\bullet}} \quad  20x + 15x^2 &= {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{5}} \times 4 \times {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{x}} + {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{5}} \times 3 \times {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{x}} \times x &\\
&= \color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{5x}(4 + 3x) &\end{align*}
$$
</div><div class="math-display" style="display:inline-block; vertical-align:top; width:calc(49% - 10px); margin:0 5px 5px 0;">
$$
\begin{align*}
\quad \textcolor{#EB7F73}{\bullet} \quad  28y - 7 &= {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{7}} \times 4y - {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{7}} \times 1 &\\
&= {\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{7}}(4y - 1) &\end{align*}
$$
</div>

</div>
</div>

<div class="environment exemple">
<div class="environment-title">Exemples</div>
<div class="environment-content">

<div style="display:inline-block; width:calc(50% - 1rem); vertical-align:top;">
<ul>
<li>
$A={{x}}{\color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{(x-4)}}-{{5}}{ \color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{(x-4)}}$<br>
$\phantom{A}={ \color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{(x-4)}}{ {(x-5)}}$
</li>
</ul>
</div>

<div style="display:inline-block; width:calc(50% - 1rem); vertical-align:top;">
<ul>
<li>
$A={ \color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{(2x-4)}}{ {(5x-3)}}-{ \color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{(2x-4)}}{ {(2x+2)}}$<br>
$\phantom{A}={ \color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{(2x-4)}}{ {(5x-3-(2x+2))}}$<br>
$\phantom{A}={ \color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{(2x-4)}}{ {(5x-3-2x-2)}}$<br>
$\phantom{A}={ \color{#EB7F73}\boldsymbol{(2x-4)}}{ {(3x-5)}}$
</li>
</ul>
</div>

</div>
</div>


<h2 class="subsection-title">1.2 Double distributivité</h2>

<div class="environment propriete">
<div class="environment-title">Propriété :</div>
<div class="environment-content">
On considère $a, b, c, d$ quatre nombres.

$$(a + b)(c + d) = ac + ad + bc + bd$$
</div>
</div>

<div class="environment exemple">
<div class="environment-title">Exemples</div>
<div class="environment-content">
<div class="math-display">
$$
\begin{align*}
\quad\quad \textcolor{#EB7F73}{\bullet} \quad (2x + 5)(3x + 7) &= 2x \times 3x + 2x \times 7 + 5 \times 3x + 5 \times 7 &\\
&= 6x^2 + 14x + 15x + 35 &\\
&= 6x^2 + 29x + 35 &
\end{align*}
$$
</div>

<div class="math-display">
$$
\begin{align*}
\quad\quad \textcolor{#EB7F73}{\bullet} \quad (3x - 5)^2 &= (3x - 5)(3x - 5) &\\
&= 3x \times 3x + 3x \times (-5) + (-5) \times 3x + (-5) \times (-5) &\\
&= 9x^2 - 15x - 15x + 25 &\\
&= 9x^2 - 30x + 25 &
\end{align*}
$$
</div>

<div class="math-display">
$$
\begin{align*}
\quad\quad \textcolor{#EB7F73}{\bullet} \quad (2y - 3)(y - 5) &= 2y \times y + 2y \times (-5) + (-3) \times y + (-3) \times (-5) &\\
&= 2y^2 - 10y - 3y + 15 &\\
&= 2y^2 - 13y + 15 &
\end{align*}
$$
</div>
</div>
</div>
